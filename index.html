About Frogs

Frogs are amphibians known for their jumping abilities, croaking sounds, and moist skin. They live in a variety of environments, from rainforests to deserts.

Types of Frogs

Tree Frogs

x

Tree frogs live in trees and have sticky pads on their toes for climbing.

Bullfrogs

x

Bullfrogs are large and in charge frogs commonly found in ponds and lakes.

x

Fun Facts

  • Frogs have permeable skin, meaning they can absorb liquid and gases through their skin. This is how they primarily "drink" water!
  • Frogs use their eyeballs to help them eat! When a frog swallows food, it pulls its eyes down into the roof of its mouth to help push food down their throats.
  • Like all amphibians, frogs are cold-blooded, meaning their body temperatures change with the temperature of their surroundings.
  • There are over 6,000 species of frogs worldwide!
